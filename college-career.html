College & Career

Balancing classes, projects, and future career goals can feel overwhelming. On this page, I share how I plan my weeks, stay on top of deadlines, and still make room for content and fitness.

How I Structure My Week

I treat my week like a puzzle: classes and exams are the non-negotiables, then I plug in work, content, and gym time around them. Time blocking in a digital calendar helps me see everything at a glance and avoid overbooking myself.

  • Blocking out class times and study sessions first.
  • Reserving certain days or hours for editing and filming.
  • Leaving small pockets of buffer time for rest or catch-up.

Weekly Essentials

Some tools I rely on to stay organized:

  • Digital calendar for classes and due dates
  • Notes app for ideas and to-do lists
  • Simple planner for a weekly overview

Managing Assignments & Deadlines

I keep a running list of upcoming assignments and exams, broken down into smaller tasks: reading, research, drafts, and final submissions. This makes big projects less scary and easier to fit into busy days.

Career Growth While in School

Between classes and content, I still try to build my future career. That includes updating my resume, working on portfolio projects, and saying yes to opportunities that align with my goals.

I use my experiences as a content creator and student as proof that I can manage real-world responsibilities, adapt to change, and stay organized.
